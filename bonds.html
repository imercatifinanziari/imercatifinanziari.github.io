BONDS

Bond is a debt instrument issued by a state (government bond) or a company (corporate bond) to raise financial resources. These securities give the holder the right to redeem the principal lent to the issuer on maturity of the security by receiving coupons.
The bonds are divided into:

  • bonds with fixed coupon rates
  • bonds with variable coupon rates
  • mixed coupon rate bonds
  • structured bonds (inflation-based interest or other market indices)

How do I purchase a bond?

Government bonds are issued through periodic auctions can be purchased by specialists, banks or investment companies appointed by the treasury. The issuer determines the duration and rates, but the price will be determined by the demands of the intermediaries. Instead, corporate bonds are issued by a pool of banks.

Italian Government Bonds can be:

BOTS

(ordinary treasury bills) are short-term bonds with a maturity of less than one year without coupons. The remuneration is determined by the difference between the redemption value and the issue price.
The lower the price of the B0T today, the more you’ll earn when the bond expires.
Example:
nominal value 100
purchase price 95
gain 5

BTPS

(multi-year treasury bills) are long-term and can be up to 30 years, have half-yearly coupons that are paid every 6 months. The coupon rate is the annual interest rate used to calculate the value of the coupon.
Example:
nominal value 100
coupon rate 5%
(100*5%/2)=coupon 2,5 each semester
100= nominal value
5%= coupon rate
2= semesters in a year

CCTS

(Treasury Credit Certificates) have maturities of 7 years, floating half-yearly coupon at market interest rate.

Bonds risks

The main risk is the failure or DEFAULT of the issuer. Many companies are in default when they cannot meet their obligations with those who own the bonds.

Rating

The default risk is assessed by the rating agencies which assign a rating based on the solvency of the issuer. (AAA, AA…BBB, BBB-)…
The most well-known rating agencies are:
-STANDARD& POOR’S
-MOODY’S
-FITCH
