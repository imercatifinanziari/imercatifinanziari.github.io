Financial markets

Financial markets are markets where those with unused savings transfer them to those who need money. For example, households accumulate savings and invest them in financial instruments allowing states to provide services and investments, or households use these resources by setting up a mortgage. Markets can be:
-PRIMARY MARKET: investors buy newly issued securities
-SECONDARY MARKET: investors buy securities already issued and present on the market.

Why are financial markets important?

They are important because they bring in resources from those who have excess to those who need them to finance productive activities such as building bridges, buying new machinery and developing new research. For the small entrepreneur they are an opportunity to realize an investment and to increase their wealth.

How does the money transfer take place?

It can be through the intermediation of a bank with the deposit of savings with a bank that lends them to an enterprise; or by the exchange of a financial instrument such as shares, bonds and derivatives through authorized operators such as banks, mutual funds of investment, brokerage firm. In summary, I can transfer my savings by depositing them into a bank account or by purchasing a security such as shares or bonds.
